Tribute to Ernie Baron

Ernie Baron in TV Patrol since 1990.

Introduction

Ernie Baron, famously known as “The Walking Encyclopedia,” was a beloved Filipino weatherman, broadcaster, and science advocate. He spent his career making science and knowledge accessible to millions of Filipinos, leaving behind a lasting legacy as one of the most influential figures in Philippine media.

Early Life

Ernie Baron was born on August 15, 1940, in Bacoor, Cavite, Philippines. From a young age, he exhibited a keen interest in science and knowledge. This passion guided him throughout his career as he made it his mission to educate the public, especially about weather, health, and general knowledge.

Career Highlights

Baron began his career in radio broadcasting during the 1960s, where his deep voice and expertise quickly earned him recognition. He joined ABS-CBN in 1972, becoming the network’s most popular weatherman. His simple and easy-to-understand weather explanations made him a household name. As a host of the radio show "Knowledge Power," he gained further fame by answering science and health-related queries from the public.

Notable Achievements

  • Weather Reporting: Ernie Baron was the primary weather anchor for ABS-CBN’s flagship news program TV Patrol for many years. His accurate weather forecasts helped millions of Filipinos prepare for daily life and natural disasters.
  • Science Advocacy: Known as the "Walking Encyclopedia," Baron authored several books and used his radio program to share practical science knowledge with ordinary people.
  • "Knowledge Power": His radio show, which also carried the slogan "Knowledge is power," became highly popular as it educated the public on science, health, and wellness.
  • Publications: Baron wrote numerous books aimed at making science and health information accessible to everyone. These publications covered topics such as home remedies, science trivia, and environmental awareness.

"Knowledge is Power."

Legacy

Ernie Baron passed away on January 23, 2006, at the age of 65. However, his influence remains strong in the hearts of many Filipinos. His dedication to making knowledge accessible, particularly in the fields of science and weather, inspired generations of broadcasters, educators, and science advocates.
